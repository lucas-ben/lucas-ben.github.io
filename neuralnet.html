Building Trust Profiles for AI in Healthcare

Author: Lucas Benjamin

In this project, we explore how individuals perceive AI's role in healthcare. By analyzing survey data on attitudes, knowledge, and demographics, we aim to construct “trust profiles” — clusters of respondents with similar patterns of comfort, concern, and optimism about AI in medical contexts.

Data Overview

The data, sourced from a Pew Research Center survey, includes self-reported comfort with AI in healthcare, perceived risks (e.g., bias, data security), and demographic information like education, race, and ideology.

Dimensionality Reduction via Factor Analysis

To reduce noise and uncover latent themes in the data, we applied Factor Analysis. This yielded two primary components:

  • F1: Positive Expectancy — trust in AI’s benefits, including quality improvements and error reduction.
  • F2: Relational Impact — concern over AI depersonalizing care or harming doctor-patient relationships.

We retained components with eigenvalues greater than 1, as shown below in the scree plot:

Scree Plot

Clustering Trust Profiles

We performed KMeans clustering using the extracted factor scores. The resulting clusters reflect distinct trust profiles:

  • Optimists — High on Positive Expectancy, low concern about relational impact.
  • Skeptics — Low expectancy, high relational concern.

Interpretation

These factors may map onto real-world personas. For instance:

  • The Tech-Savvy Patient: High AI knowledge, high comfort, minimal concern.
  • The Cautious Realist: Balanced expectations, but high privacy concerns.
  • The Humanist Skeptic: Low expectancy, high concern over depersonalization.

Next Steps

These trust profiles will serve as the foundation for predictive models that can anticipate future attitudes or behaviors based on demographic and psychographic inputs.

Stay tuned as we build a machine learning model to classify new respondents using these factor-derived profiles.
