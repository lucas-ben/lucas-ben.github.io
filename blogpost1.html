Why Staying Invested During the Trump Trade War Is Still the Best Move

By Lucas Ben April 4, 2025 3 min read

The resurgence of Donald Trump's trade war rhetoric has rattled markets once again. With threats of sweeping tariffs on Chinese goods, potential European auto tariffs, and an "America First" economic policy, investors are understandably nervous. But history and statistics tell us one thing clearly: the best decision is to stay invested.

U.S. Exceptionalism Isn't Going Anywhere

Despite trade tensions, the U.S. economy remains the most resilient in the world. American markets have weathered far worse—financial crises, inflation shocks, and even previous trade wars.

Trying to predict market movements in such turbulent times is no better a strategy than gambling with your money. The U.S. still leads in innovation, corporate earnings, and global financial dominance. If you pull out now, you risk making emotional decisions that could undermine your long-term financial goals.

If You're Nervous, Diversify—But Don't Exit

For those worried about overexposure to U.S. volatility, the answer isn't to cash out—it's to diversify intelligently. Consider:

  • Chinese Market ETFs (e.g., MCHI, FXI): Despite tensions, China's economy is still growing, and its tech and manufacturing sectors offer value.
  • European Market ETFs (e.g., VGK, IEV): Europe's stable dividend-paying companies provide a hedge against U.S.-centric risks.

Diversification smooths out returns without forcing you to time the market—a losing strategy for most investors.

The Biggest Risk? Sitting on the Sidelines

Market timing fails overwhelmingly. Missing just a few of the best trading days can devastate long-term returns. Staying fully invested—even through turbulence—has always been the statistically superior approach.

Bottom Line:
The Trump trade war may bring volatility, but U.S. markets will endure. If you're anxious, diversify—don't retreat. History rewards those who stay patient.

Stay long, stay strong.
