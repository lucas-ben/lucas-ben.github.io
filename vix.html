VIX-SPX Relationship: Decoding Market Volatility

By Lucas Ben | March 2025 | 5 min read

The relationship between the S&P 500 (SPX) and the CBOE Volatility Index (VIX) represents one of the most fundamental dynamics in financial markets. This analysis explores their inverse correlation, volatility characteristics, and potential trading strategies based on mean reversion.

The Volatility Index (VIX)

Often called the "fear gauge," the VIX measures the market's expectation of 30-day volatility derived from SPX option prices. Key characteristics:

Mean Level

21.03 (long-term average)

Standard Deviation

8.16

Historical Range

11.54 to 82.69

Figure 1: Historical comparison showing the inverse relationship between VIX (right axis) and SPX (left axis)

The Inverse Correlation

The VIX and SPX exhibit a strong negative correlation (-0.73 based on our analysis). This relationship emerges because:

  • During market declines, uncertainty rises → VIX increases
  • During rallies, confidence grows → VIX decreases
  • Options demand shifts create this relationship

Correlation Analysis

Mean Reversion Trading Strategies

Both instruments show strong mean-reverting tendencies, creating potential trading opportunities:

SPX Strategy

When SPX deviates >10% from its 200-day moving average:

  • Buy when below the average
  • Sell/short when above
Figure 3: SPX with buy (green) and sell (red) signals based on mean reversion

VIX Strategy

When VIX spikes >25% above its 200-day moving average:

  • Short VIX futures or buy inverse VIX ETFs
  • Exit when VIX returns to its mean
Figure 4: VIX with trading signals based on deviation from 200-day MA

Key Takeaways

Negative Correlation

The -0.73 correlation confirms VIX rises when SPX falls

Mean Reversion

Both series consistently revert to their long-term averages

Trading Edge

Suggests a pairs trading strategy

Implementation Note

These strategies work best when:

  • Combined with proper risk management
  • Implemented during normal volatility regimes
  • Used as part of a diversified portfolio
